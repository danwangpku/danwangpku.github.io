I am a third year master student in Institute of Environmental Sciences, Peking University studying the distribution of neonicotinoid pesticide in different compartments and the degradation pathways. I previously completed my Bachelor’s in Environmental Sciences, Peking university in July, 2014.

As an environmental student, I have a broad background of basic knowledge, include biology, chemistry, geography and some economics. And my strongest interest lies on bioremediation and toxicity. Also, environmental epidemiology interests me a lot.

During my undergraduate time, I did some lab works with PAHs, including sample preparation and GC-MS analyzing work, and I also collected human exposure samples in common people. I went to the US for a 5-months internship in Oregon State university working in Staci Sminonich’s lab. There I participated in some toxicity experiments, like Ames assay and zebrafish toxicity test.

After that I’m been working on biodegradation, first on 6-2 FTOH, followed by neonicotinoid. We used DDGE and stable isotope labeling methods to trace the biodegradation process. I want to learn more about microbes and remediation.

In addition, for a strong personal desire, I took a lot courses of cancer, about its molecular signal pathways and the genetics and biochemistry when I was an undergraduate student. After that, I learned two key courses about environmental exposure and environmental epidemiology. I find epidemiology is so fascinating, even so I never did some works on it before but I have a quiet wide background knowledge that leaves me no barriers to understand the molecular mechanism and the exposure measurement. I make several experiment designs in class, one is about the public exposure to the pesticides I have been studying. The professor asked if I want to work it out, I hesitated since it’s unrelated to what I’m been doing, but I just can’t forget about it and grow more interested in this new field for me.

It’s my third year as a master student and I'll apply for a Ph.D. degree on biodegradation or epidemiology in universities of America. Please feel free to contact me if you think I may be qualified for you.
