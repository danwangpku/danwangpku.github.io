During my first year as a master student, I focus on the computer science courses for master students and get better scores than most students whose bachelor major is computer science. Besides, I learn machine learning and NLP foundations not only from school courses, but also from books and online courses. I get my machine learning basics from Andrew NG’s online course, Bishop’s book Pattern Recognition and Machine Learning and Larry Wasserman’s book All of Statistics. All of them are best materials and I reviewed those materials several times and learnt more every time I did it. Bishop’s book is my favorite because it gives detailed formula derivation which makes me feel delving into those theories. Maybe also because both of us have physics background:).

I’m in desperate interests in deep learning since October, 2014 and I catch up with the newest progresses through arxiv, DeepMind and Deep Learning Community. I firstly learn deep learning mainly from deep learning tutorial and from Hinton’s deep learning course. Additionally, I read the original papers for the specific models like DQN, LSTM, RBM etc. I read deep learning related papers from conferences including NIPS, ICML, ACL, EMNLP, COLING, NAACL, TACL, ICLR etc. I implemented many deep learning models in my github project—Machine Learning Models.
