I am a third year master's student in College of Urban and Environmental Sciences, Peking University studying the distribution of neonicotinoid pesticide in different compartments and the degradation pathways. I previously completed my bachelor degree in College of Urban and Environmental Sciences, Peking University in July, 2014.

I believe that a good knowledge foundation is the premise to be a qualified researcher. As an environmental student, I have a broad background of basic knowledge, including biology, chemistry, geography and some economics. And my strongest interest lies on bioremediation and toxicity.

I have been working on biodegradation, first on 6-2 FTOH, followed by neonicotinoid. We used DDGE, stable isotope labeling methods and LC-MS to trace the biodegradation process. I want to learn more about microbes and remediation, especially some technical problems faced with this area and the evaluation of post remediation toxicity.

During my undergraduate time, I did some lab works on PAHs, including sample preparation and GC-MS analyzing work, and I also collected human exposure samples in common people. I went to the US for a 5-months internship in Oregon State university working in Staci Sminonich’s lab. There I participated in some toxicity experiments, like Ames assay and zebrafish toxicity test.

It’s my third year as a master student and I'll apply for a Ph.D. degree on biodegradation in universities of America. Please feel free to contact me if you think I may be qualified for you.
